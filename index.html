03 — What I Believe

These aren't mantras.
They're conclusions.

Discipline

Freedom is earned through structure. The people who seem most effortless have put in the most invisible work. I've made peace with that.

Attention

Where you put your attention is who you become. Most people don't choose—they react. I'm trying to choose, deliberately, every day.

Standards

Most things can be slightly better. I don't say this with anxiety. I say it with curiosity. There's almost always another layer.

Energy

Energy is the real currency. Not time. Not money. What drains you and what feeds you—that's the map you should be reading.

Growth

Discomfort and expansion feel identical at first. I've stopped trying to tell them apart—and just moved toward both.

People

I'm drawn to people who take themselves seriously without taking themselves too seriously. That balance is rarer than it should be.

On identity

I've spent years curating how I appear—not out of deception, but because I genuinely believe presentation is a form of respect. But curation has a cost. Sometimes I forget which version of me came first.

On ambition

The honest version: I don't want to be famous. I want to make things that outlast me. There's a difference. One needs an audience. The other needs courage.

On connection

I'm more guarded than I look. Not because I'm protecting myself—but because I take it seriously when someone gets close. I don't do surface level well. It bores me, and I always end up saying something too real too soon.

On the work

Most things I make, I delete. Not from doubt—from standards. The ones I keep are the ones that scared me a little when I made them. That fear is the signal.
